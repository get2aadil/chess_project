Chess: A Brief History

Chess is one of the oldest and most widely played games around the world. Itâ€™s a strategy game for two players, where both skill and patience are key. The game as we know it today has evolved over many centuries, but its roots go back to Northern India around the 6th century, where it was called chaturanga. From there, it spread to Persia and gained popularity. Eventually, it made its way to the Arab world and later Europe during the Middle Ages.

By the 15th century, chess had taken on the form we recognize now, with the introduction of the powerful Queen piece. Over time, chess became a global phenomenon, enjoyed by millions of people from different cultures and backgrounds. Beyond being a fun game, chess is also known for helping players develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills, which is why it remains so popular today.
